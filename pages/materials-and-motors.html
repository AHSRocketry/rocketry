Materials & Motors Available

Choose materials and motors that match your mission and keep the design stable, strong, and buildable with our tools.

Body Tubes (Estes)

Available sizes: BT‑5, BT‑20, BT‑50, BT‑55, BT‑60, BT‑70, BT‑80. Max single piece length: 18″. Longer airframes require a coupler.

  • BT‑5 / BT‑20 — small airframes (18 mm mounts fit BT‑20); great for simple, low‑mass builds.
  • BT‑50 / BT‑55 — room for sturdier mounts/recovery; BT‑50 matches 24 mm OD reasonably.
  • BT‑60 — versatile for payloads/eggs and clustering in larger variants.
  • BT‑70 / BT‑80 — larger payloads, more stable platforms; higher drag & mass—size recovery accordingly.

Fin & Structural Materials

  • 3.5 mm balsa — light; use for low loads or add paper/CA hardening; ideal for small rockets.
  • 2 mm balsa — ultralight; best for very small airframes; consider reinforcement.
  • 1/8″ plywood — stiff and durable; recommended for larger fins or cluster/stage stress.
  • 1/8″ MDF (centering rings) — laser‑cuts cleanly; seal edges if exposed; avoid high moisture.
  • Retainers: steel hooks (clip‑style) available; we can 3D print screw‑on retainers for BT‑50/BT‑55/BT‑60+ as needed.
  • Shock cords: elastic & Kevlar; use Kevlar near ejection heat, elastic to absorb landing shock.
  • Parachutes: Estes 6″, 9″, 12″, 15″, 18″, 24″ — size to mass and desired descent rate.

Motors on Hand

All motors are 18 mm diameter except the C11 series which are 24 mm.

  • A8‑3 (18 mm) — training flights, low altitude, gentle recovery timing.
  • B6‑0, B6‑4, B6‑6 (18 mm) — 0 = booster (no delay/ejection), 4/6 = delay seconds for ejection.
  • C6‑0, C6‑5, C6‑7 (18 mm) — similar timing logic; higher total impulse.
  • C11‑0, C11‑3, C11‑7 (24 mm) — larger airframes; stronger initial thrust.
  • D12‑6 (18 mm) — high thrust for heavier 18 mm builds; ensure airframe and recovery are sized appropriately.

Design notes: Match motor mount to diameter (18 mm vs 24 mm). In OpenRocket, verify thrust‑to‑weight >~ 5:1 at liftoff, safe max velocity before fin flutter, and acceptable landing speed.

Quick Selection Patterns

  • First rocket: BT‑20 or BT‑50, balsa fins (3.5 mm), A8‑3 or B6‑4, 12″–15″ chute.
  • Altitude target (~400–800 ft): BT‑50/BT‑55, thin fins (reinforced), B6‑6 or C6‑5, 12″ chute.
  • Egg payload: BT‑60/BT‑70, 1/8″ ply fins, C11‑3 or D12‑6 (check sims), 18″–24″ chute.
  • Cluster/stage: BT‑60+; reinforce fins & mounts; use appropriate booster/sustainer delays (B6‑0/C6‑0 boosters).
