Specify the Rocket’s Mission

Define what you want this rocket to do before you design anything. A clear mission prevents analysis paralysis and focuses your choices (materials, motor class, recovery, fabrication steps, and test plan).

Common Mission Profiles

  • First Rocket / Baseline Flight — single motor, simple parachute recovery; aim for a reliable, low‑stress build.
  • Altitude Target — tune mass, drag, and motor choice to hit a specific apogee (competition style).
  • Payload / Technology Demo — carry an egg, altimeter, tracker, or small sensors; plan for mounting, wiring, and access.
  • Cluster — two or more motors at liftoff; requires reliable ignition plan and added structural margin.
  • Staging — booster + sustainer; requires staging hardware, ignition transfer, and careful stability checks per stage.

Mission Definition Worksheet

  1. Objective: (baseline / altitude target / payload / cluster / staging)
  2. Primary success criteria: (e.g., “reaches 400–500 ft and recovers undamaged”)
  3. Constraints: field size, budget, build time, materials/tools, skill level, motor availability.
  4. Risk tolerance: conservative (training) ↔ aggressive (experiment/competition)
  5. Recovery plan: parachute/streamer size, attachment, packing space, ejection timing.
  6. Measurement plan (if any): altimeter/tracker/egg payload; mounting and retrieval.
  7. Launch readiness checklist items unique to this mission: (e.g., cluster igniter continuity, staging interstage fit)

Translate Mission → Design Targets

  • OpenRocket targets: stability margin (CG–CP), mass budget, projected altitude, deployment timing.
  • Geometry: body tube size(s), fin planform & thickness, nose type, couplers, bays.
  • Fabrication choices: laser‑cut fins/rings vs 3D‑printed parts, retainer type, adhesives.
  • Recovery: parachute/streamer size vs mass; shock cord material & length; attachment points.
  • Motor envelope: designed for 18 mm or 24 mm? thrust‑to‑weight & burnout speed acceptable?
