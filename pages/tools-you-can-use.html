Tools You Can Use

A quick guide to the software and shop tools available for design, fabrication, and assembly.

Software

  • OpenRocket — Simulate geometry, motors, CG/CP stability, and flight performance to de‑risk your design in minutes.
  • OnShape — Cloud CAD for parametric parts (nose cones, transitions, rings, jigs); export STL (3D print) and DXF/SVG (laser).
  • Cura Slicer + 3D Printers — Convert STL to G‑code; control layer height, walls, infill, and supports for strong, light parts.
  • Glowforge (Laser Cutter) — Precision‑cut fins, centering rings, bulkheads, and jigs from plywood/MDF; bring DXF/SVG.

Measuring, Marking, and Cutting

  • X‑Acto knives — sharp blades for clean tube cuts; make multiple light passes.
  • Length marking tools — steel rules, marking gauges for repeatable lengths.
  • Estes Ultimate Tube Marking Guide — draw straight, axial reference lines on tubes (fin lines, slot layout).
  • Estes Tube Cutting Guides — clamp‑on jigs for square, repeatable tube cuts.
  • Sandpaper & blocks — 180/220/320/600/1500 grits; shape fins (leading/trailing edges), smooth prints, prep for paint.

Adhesives & Application

  • Glue swabs — precise adhesive placement; keep joints neat.
  • Elmer’s (PVA) — paper‑to‑paper bonds (body tube to paper couplers, paper wraps).
  • Wood Glue — wood‑to‑paper (fins to paper tubes) or wood‑to‑wood; strong, slightly flexible joints.
  • CA (Super Glue) — plastic parts or quick tacks; use sparingly, beware of brittleness.
  • Epoxy — higher strength and gap‑filling; choose cure time:
    • 5‑minute — quick tacks and small parts; lower working time.
    • 15‑minute — balanced working/open time for typical assemblies.
    • 30‑minute — best strength and penetration; ideal for high‑load joints.

Tip: Dry‑fit everything first. Lightly scuff bonding surfaces. Clean before gluing.

When to Use What (Quick Map)

  • Design & Sim: OpenRocket → confirm stability & motor.
  • CAD Parts: OnShape → export STL (prints) / DXF/SVG (laser).
  • 3D Prints: Cura → nose, transitions, retainers; sand 220→600 for paint.
  • Laser: Glowforge → fins (1/8″ ply), rings (1/8″ MDF), jigs.
  • Assembly: Wood glue (fins/tubes), PVA (paper/paper), CA (plastics/spot), Epoxy (high‑load joints).
  • Finishing: 320→600→1500 grit; prime, paint, decals.
